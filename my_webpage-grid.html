Historic places in Cape Town

District Six Museum

  • It a memorial to the forced removal of 60 000 residents of various races from District Six, a former inner-city residential area during apartheid.
  • The museum floor is covered with a map of the district littered with handwritten labels from former residents showing the location of their houses.
  • It houses an impressive collection of historical materials including photographs, paintings, artefacts, physical remains like street signs, books and studies as well as audio-visual recordings of District Six, most which were donated by its former residents.

Castle of Good Hope

  • It's the oldest existing colonial building in South Africa, it was built between 166 and 1679by the Dutch East india Company.
  • The Castle was built by soldiers, volunteers, slaves and Khoi undergoing punishment.
  • it was declare a national monument in 1936.
